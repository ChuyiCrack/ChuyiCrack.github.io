// what happened

The story

I traveled to Monterrey representing Polytechnic University of Sinaloa to compete at HackMTY — Mexico's largest student hackathon, hosted at Tecnológico de Monterrey. Hundreds of teams from across the country competed across multiple company challenges.

We took on Capital One's fintech challenge, which pushed us to build a product that made personal finance more engaging for young users. In 24 hours, we had to design, build, and ship a fully working app — not a mockup, not a prototype, a live demo.

Our idea: a gamified savings app where users grow a virtual plant as they hit their savings milestones. The more consistent and disciplined you are with saving money, the more your plant grows and thrives. Simple concept, but it struck the right chord — personal finance tools are boring, so we made one that isn't.

I led the team through the full sprint — from initial concept to final demo — coordinating tasks, keeping everyone unblocked through the night, and presenting the live working product to Capital One industry judges at the end.
